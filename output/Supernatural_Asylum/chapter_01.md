# Chapter 1: Echoes of the Past

Dr. Evelyn Carter stood at the rusted gates of the St. Margery's Asylum, an imposing relic from the turn of the century, now overrun by nature and the passage of time. Vines crept over its stone façade like skeletal fingers, hinting at the building's sinister past. The wind whistled through the broken windows, carrying whispers and lamentations from another era.

Evelyn wasn’t a stranger to silence, nor was she easily perturbed, but a shiver traversed her spine nonetheless. As a professor of parapsychology, she had visited countless sites imbued with tragedy, yet there was something about this place that felt intensely personal and unnervingly familiar.

“Are you sure about this?” her colleague, Thomas Hale, asked as he rummaged through the trunk of their van for equipment. His voice was tinged with skepticism that belied his own curiosity. Thomas had been her partner in many investigations, a steadfast companion.

“Yes, Thomas. This place has a story that’s begging to be told,” Evelyn replied, her eyes fixated on the weathered building. She took a deep breath, tasting the metallic tang in the air, a mingling of old iron and forgotten sorrow.

They made their way up the cobblestone path, the crunch of gravel beneath their feet the only interruption in the oppressive silence. As they neared the entrance, the massive oak doors, despite their age, opened with an unsettling ease, as if welcoming them into the abyss.

Inside, the asylum was as one might expect—dark corridors that stretched into obscurity, dilapidated rooms filled with decaying furniture, and walls stained by time and unspeakable events. The chill of the interior was pervasive, seeping through their clothing and into their very bones.

Their flashlights sliced through the gloom, revealing glimpses of old medical records scattered across the floor, faded photographs of nurses and patients pinned to corkboards, all silent witnesses to the asylum’s history.

Evelyn paused at what once might have been the reception desk. There was a faded ledger, its pages crumbling at her touch, documenting admissions from decades past. Men and women labeled with conditions that modern psychiatry would have treated with far more compassion and understanding.

“This place... it feels alive,” Thomas remarked softly, his voice echoing against the walls covered in peeling mustard-colored paint.

“Alive and waiting,” Evelyn replied, a thoughtful frown on her face. “The stories of what happened here have never been adequately told. It’s as if a part of it still lingers, seeking acknowledgement.”

A sudden cool breeze swept through the hallway, carrying a faint melody. A child’s lullaby, distorted and out of place, caused both investigators to halt, their eyes meeting in shared bewilderment.

“Did you hear that?” Evelyn whispered, straining to follow the ethereal notes that seemed to emanate from the depths of the asylum.

“Yes,” Thomas said, his grip tightening on his flashlight, the beam wavering momentarily. “It’s like the past is speaking to us.”

Evelyn nodded, determined to uncover whatever secrets St. Margery's held within its shadowy embrace. The melody lingered, weaving through the air like an invisible thread, drawing them deeper into the asylum—into its heart where madness and memory converged.

Evelyn knew they were only at the beginning of their journey, and whatever lay ahead, imbued with otherworldly presence, would test the limits of her understanding and sanity.

## Author Note:
This chapter introduces Dr. Evelyn Carter and Thomas Hale as they begin their exploration of an abandoned asylum, charged with supernatural echoes from its infamous past. Careful steps are taken to ensure the necessary atmosphere for a psychological horror novel, emphasizing the manipulation of perceptual norms and the unsettling crossing of thresholds into the unknown. The chapter foreshadows the ominous elements they will confront, establishing a basis for the supernatural theme to unfold.
