# Echoes of the Past  

Dr. Evelyn Carter stood at the entrance of St. Margery's Asylum, her eyes tracing the aged facade of the Gothic building. The asylum loomed in the grey afternoon light, its spires clawing at the overcast sky. Beside her, Thomas Hale adjusted his glasses, his eyes squinting as if trying to pierce the mysteries that lay beyond the moss-covered walls.

"It hasn't changed a bit," Evelyn muttered, feeling the weight of history in the air—a foreboding that seemed to seep from the very stones of the asylum. 

Thomas chuckled, though it lacked humor. "One would think the years have passed in vain," he said, brushing a fleck of dust from his coat. "But it's the very atmosphere we're here to explore, isn't it?"

As experts in paranormal activity, Evelyn and Thomas had seen their fair share of hauntings, but St. Margery's promised to be unlike anything they had encountered. Built in the late 1800s, it had long been decommissioned, its tales of madness and spectral sightings relegated to the annals of local legend.

The two entered the asylum, the door creaking ominously as it swung inward. The air was stale, thick with dust and decay, interrupted only by the distant sound of dripping water echoing through the corridors. As they moved deeper inside, the shadows seemed to cling to them, conduits of the past whispering forgotten secrets.

"Did you hear that?" Thomas asked, his voice a low murmur.

Evelyn paused, her heart quickening. Faintly, almost imperceptibly, a melody drifted through the halls. It was a lullaby, sung in a voice so soft it seemed to blend seamlessly with the very air.

"I hear it," Evelyn confirmed, her pulse thrumming in her ears. "It's as if the walls remember..."

Their footsteps echoed hollowly as they followed the sound, each step drawing them further into the asylum's embrace. The ward was a labyrinth of memories, the remnants of its inhabitants' anguish etched into the walls and ceilings. Doors hung ajar, revealing rooms filled with decrepit furniture, a testament to the passage of time and the weight of abandonment.

Suddenly, the song ceased, leaving a chilling silence in its wake. It was then that Evelyn noticed the photograph—a black-and-white image hanging askew on the wall. It depicted a group of patients and staff, their expressions ghostly under the flash of an old camera.

Thomas moved closer, his breath misting in the cold. "Look," he said, pointing at the figure in the corner of the photo—a nurse, her eyes dark and intense, staring directly into the camera lens.

"Who is she?" Evelyn wondered aloud, feeling an inexplicable connection to the woman. Her gaze swept over the room, half-expecting the figure to step out from the shadows.

As if in response, the lullaby began again, more distinct this time, weaving through the corridors like a thread pulling them inexorably deeper into the asylum’s heart.

"We should document this," Thomas suggested, reaching for his camera. "This place is alive with echoes."

Evelyn nodded, her resolve firming. "Let's uncover these stories," she said, stepping back into the corridor where the air was thick with potentiality.

And so, with their equipment in tow, they embarked on the precarious path between history and haunting, determined to uncover the secrets of St. Margery's Asylum. The echoes of the past seemed almost eager to share their tales.

As the chapter comes to a close, the haunting melody fades away, leaving Evelyn and Thomas with the realization that the asylum is more than just a relic of the past—it's an entity, watching and waiting.
